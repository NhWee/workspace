Blender Mantaflow FLIP wave tank
================================

Purpose
-------
This is the main high-realism path for the project. It replaces the browser
surface approximation with Blender's Mantaflow liquid solver, which uses a
FLIP-style liquid domain and can bake liquid mesh plus foam, bubble, and spray
secondary particles.

Scene
-----
The generated scene contains:

1. an 18 x 6 x 5.5 liquid domain
2. an initial liquid volume
3. a hidden animated paddle that injects uneven wave-group energy
4. a sloped absorbing beach that reduces hard-wall reflection
5. multiple collision rocks that create breaking flow and splash
6. liquid mesh and foam/bubble/spray settings
7. Cycles/Filmic render settings for more realistic water previews
8. foam and spray render proxy emitters around the main breaking zones
9. an annular vortex foam emitter around the rock zone

Quality
-------
preview: resolution 112 for iteration.
long_preview: resolution 96, 360 frames, 48 fps. This is for a longer and
faster-to-watch driven wave tank preview.
ocean_preview: resolution 144, 300 frames, 30 fps. This is the preferred
iteration preset for the improved physical wave tank.
production: resolution 256, higher mesh scale, and more time substeps. This is
an offline bake and may take a long time depending on cache frames and GPU.

Create a preview scene
----------------------
From the project root:

  .\tools\blender-5.1.2\blender-5.1.2-windows-x64\blender.exe --background --python wave_sim\blender_fluid\create_mantaflow_wave_tank.py -- --quality preview

Create and bake a longer fast-playback preview
----------------------------------------------

  .\tools\blender-5.1.2\blender-5.1.2-windows-x64\blender.exe --background --python wave_sim\blender_fluid\create_mantaflow_wave_tank.py -- --quality long_preview --output outputs\mantaflow_wave_tank_long_preview.blend --cache-dir outputs\mantaflow_wave_tank_long_preview_cache --bake

Create and bake the improved ocean-style preview
------------------------------------------------

  .\tools\blender-5.1.2\blender-5.1.2-windows-x64\blender.exe --background --python wave_sim\blender_fluid\create_mantaflow_wave_tank.py -- --quality ocean_preview --output outputs\mantaflow_wave_tank_ocean_preview.blend --cache-dir outputs\mantaflow_wave_tank_ocean_preview_cache --bake

Optional overrides:

  --frame-end 480
  --fps 60

Higher fps makes the same baked frames play faster in Blender. Higher
frame-end makes the simulation longer, but also increases bake time and cache
size.

What is now considered
----------------------

The improved scene now considers:

1. 3D FLIP liquid volume and pressure solve through Mantaflow
2. gravity and a conservative CFL setting
3. repeated external forcing from a hidden wave paddle
4. uneven wave groups from mixed paddle periods
5. solid collision with rocks, tank floor, side walls, and a sloped beach
6. secondary foam, bubble, spray, and tracer particles
7. higher vorticity to preserve visible rotational motion
8. lower surface tension for more active breaking behavior
9. Cycles render settings, Filmic tone mapping, and denoising
10. visible foam/spray render proxy particles near rocks and the absorbing
    beach, used when Mantaflow's native secondary cache is sparse in short
    preview bakes
11. stronger mesh smoothing and a render smoothing modifier for softer preview
    surfaces
12. a stable vortex-foam ring around the breaking zone. Strong hidden vortex
    collision drivers were tested but removed because they produced unrealistic
    vertical water columns in short bakes.

Still to improve
----------------

The next realism gaps are:

1. Native Mantaflow secondary particles can still be sparse in short bakes.
   The proxy emitters make foam visible, but a longer/high-resolution bake is
   needed to tune this against the actual liquid motion.
2. The absorbing beach is a geometric approximation, not a perfect open ocean
   boundary condition.
3. The water material is still simple. A better shader should separate deep
   water, thin crests, foam, and spray.
4. Full production bakes should use longer frame ranges and higher resolution,
   but they will create large local cache folders.
5. A physically coupled vortex should be revisited later with a proper velocity
   field or higher-resolution FLIP setup. The current version keeps the liquid
   stable and shows the rotating region through foam placement.

Create and bake the production scene
------------------------------------

  .\tools\blender-5.1.2\blender-5.1.2-windows-x64\blender.exe --background --python wave_sim\blender_fluid\create_mantaflow_wave_tank.py -- --quality production --bake

The blend scene is written to outputs/mantaflow_wave_tank.blend and its cache
is written to outputs/mantaflow_wave_tank_cache/.
